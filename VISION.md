# Skill-Based Dancing Penguin Game

Create a playful arcade rhythm game where the player helps a penguin perform dances by timing inputs accurately, building skill through repeatable challenges, visible feedback, and improving scores.

## Who it serves
Casual game players who like quick skill-based challenges, Players who enjoy rhythm, timing, and mastery loops, Younger or broad-audience players attracted to a funny penguin dance theme

## Optimize toward
A complete playable browser game centered on a dancing penguin rhythm challenge. The core loop is fast to understand, timing-based, replayable, and clearly rewards player skill. The finished version should feel like an actual small game, not a static animation, demo screen, or generic template.

## Accept work that
- Start a dance round: the player chooses or begins a song/challenge and immediately sees the penguin, beat track, input prompts, score, combo, and progress through the routine.
- Match the rhythm: the player presses the shown directions or dance moves in time with the beat, receiving instant feedback such as Perfect, Good, Miss, combo changes, and penguin animation changes.
- Recover from mistakes: when the player misses notes, the game clearly shows the miss, breaks or reduces combo, keeps the round moving, and lets the player regain momentum through accurate timing.
- Finish and review performance: at the end of a routine, the player sees score, accuracy, max combo, rank, and simple breakdown of what to improve.
- Replay for mastery: the player can quickly restart the same routine to beat their score or choose a harder routine with denser timing patterns.
- Experience penguin personality: the penguin should visibly dance, celebrate strong play, stumble or react to misses, and feel like the central character without distracting from the skill challenge.

## Close work that
- Do not make the game a passive dancing penguin animation
- Do not make gameplay random or luck-based
- Do not bury the game behind a landing page, instructions page, or setup flow
- Do not overload the screen with decorative UI that makes timing prompts hard to read
- Do not use internal product, repository, or automation terminology in the customer-facing game

## Keep it feeling
- Playful, bright, and kinetic, with the penguin as the first visual focus
- Arcade-rhythm feel: clear beat cues, readable prompts, immediate feedback, satisfying combo and score changes
- Simple enough to understand in seconds, but strict enough that better timing produces visibly better results
- Cute character presentation supporting a genuine skill challenge
